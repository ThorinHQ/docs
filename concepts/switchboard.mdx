title: "Switchboard"

---

Switchboard is your personal review queue. When your agent picks up an email and decides on an action, that action lands in Switchboard so you can approve, edit, or decline it before anything ships.

## What it is

Switchboard is a per-user surface. Each row represents one email thread your agent looked at and the action it intends to take — reply to the sender, route the thread to a teammate, open a ticket, update a record, and so on.

For each row you see:

- The original email thread.
- The agent's reasoning for the action it picked.
- The proposed action itself, ready to inspect.

## When it appears

The **Switchboard** sidebar item only shows up for users with at least one **screenable inbox** — that is, a mailbox configured so the agent's actions need human review before they ship. If you have unread items, the sidebar entry shows an unread badge with the count.

<Note>
  If you don't see Switchboard in the sidebar, your agent isn't currently set to screen any inbox. An owner can change that on the agent.
</Note>

## Reviewing an action

Open a row to inspect the email and the proposed action. From there you have three choices:

- **Approve** — ship the action as proposed.
- **Edit and approve** — adjust the action (for example, tweak a draft reply) and then ship it.
- **Decline** — reject the action. The agent will not take it.

Once an action is approved or declined, it moves out of the active queue and into the **actioned** panel, where it stays for audit.

## Routebook

The **Routebook** is a per-user document of standing instructions your agent reads when deciding what to do. Use it to capture the rules of thumb you'd give a new assistant — who to loop in for which topics, which threads to never touch, the tone to use with specific contacts.

Open the Routebook from the Routebook sheet in Switchboard. Changes take effect on the agent's next session.

<Tip>
  When you find yourself editing the same kind of action over and over in Switchboard, that's a sign to add an instruction to your Routebook.
</Tip>

## Snooze

If a thread isn't ready to act on yet, you can snooze it instead of approving or declining. Snoozed threads disappear from the active queue and come back later for another look.

## Contacts

Switchboard also has a contacts dialog where you can review and edit your agent's allowed contacts without leaving the review surface. Use it to add a new recipient your agent should be able to message, or to tighten permissions on someone the agent is currently allowed to reach.

See [Agents](/concepts/agents) for how contacts and permission grants work in more depth.
