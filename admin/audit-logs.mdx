---
title: "Audit logs"
description: "Inspect every mutating action in your tenant."
---

Audit logs capture every action that changed persisted state in your Thorin tenant — who did it, what they touched, and when. They live at **Admin → Trust Center → Audit Logs** and are visible only to admins.

## What gets logged

Any user-, agent-, or system-initiated action that mutates state shows up here. That includes:

- Configuration changes (email access mode, deny rules, role changes).
- Integration installs, updates, and removals.
- Automation edits and skill changes.
- Agent and resource updates.
- User invites, role assignments, and removals.

Read-only actions — viewing a thread, opening Switchboard — are not logged.

## What each entry shows

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Timestamp** | When the action happened. |
| **Actor** | Who initiated it: a user, an agent, or the system. |
| **Resource type** | What kind of thing was changed (agent, integration, policy…). |
| **Resource id** | The specific record that was touched. |
| **Action** | The verb — `create`, `update`, `delete`, and friends. |
| **Summary** | A human-readable line describing the change. |

## Filtering

The toolbar above the table lets you narrow the view by:

- **Actor type** — user, agent, or system.
- **Specific actor** — a member picker scoped to the actor type you chose.
- **Resource type** — only show changes to a particular kind of record.
- **Free-text search** — matches across summaries and resource ids.

Filters compose, so you can ask for "every change agent X made to integrations last week" by combining actor + resource type + the date implied by where you scroll.

## Infinite scroll

The table loads in pages. Older entries appear automatically as you scroll down — there are no page numbers to click through.

<Tip>
  Pair audit logs with [email access](/admin/email-access) policies when investigating a delivery you didn't expect. The audit trail tells you what changed and when; the email access policy tells you what Thorin was allowed to read at that moment.
</Tip>
