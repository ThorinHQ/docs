Thorin is a workbench for company agents. Use it to chat with a personal agent, build project agents, connect business systems, organize company context, and automate recurring work.

This wiki explains the product at [app.thorin.com](https://app.thorin.com): how the pieces fit together, how to set up users and integrations, and how to build workflows such as deal flow, deal prep briefs, and inbox switchboards.

## How Thorin works

Thorin has six main layers:

1. Agents that reason and act. Every user has a personal agent, and teams can create project agents for shared workflows.
2. Feeds and resources that narrow private systems into useful, permissioned context.
3. Entities that store durable business state such as deals, accounts, customers, or candidates.
4. Skills and automations that teach repeatable behavior and run it on schedules or events.
5. Form factors such as web chat, email, Slack, SMS, and background automations.
6. Work items and sessions that make output accountable and inspectable.
